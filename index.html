"Unknown" Terrorist attacks on the Rise

As the number of attacks on U.S. soil skyrockets, so have the unknowns.

By Sawyer Click

Published July 19, 2019

"Unknown" attackers have been plaguing the Global Terrorism Database for years. Attacks have jumped higher than ever in the last few years.

In its last few years, the database recorded a heightened number of total attacks, with more unknown attacks than ever. The increase of total attacks has been ongoing since 2011, when there were 10 attacks. In 2017, the most current year in the database, there were 65, over a 650% increase from 2011. The largest jump happened from 2015 to 2016.

Unknown attacks have been up and down from year to year but have been on the rise.

The dataset's codebook defines unknown attackers as an attack where no group clearly claimed orchestrating it. Some attacks are noted as having possible attackers, but START determined it to be inconclusive.

Overall, the number of attacks from unidentified groups is taking up more and more of each year's attacks. The 9-year-average is up over 10%, thanks primarily to the craters from 2000-08 and the peaks in 2009-17.

Places of worship have been the number one target for the past three years for unknown attackers, likely because of a growing white nationalism movement in the U.S. that has divided the country.

For 2015, the most popular motive recorded by START speculates the attacks were against the Muslim community. For 2016, the most popular motive speculates the attacks stem from the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant. Both motives were carried out from unidentified groups, which more than doubled from 2015 to 2016 and made up about half of all attacks in both years.

The attack on places of worship by unknown attackers peaked in 2016 with 17 attacks. The following year saw a peak in attacks on places of worship by known attackers with 10 attacks.

Looking at the past three years geographically, both unknown and known attacks have taken place in areas with high populations, as expected: Texas, California, Florida and New York. Florida, however, had a significant amount of attacks from known attackers and only 3 from known attackers. On the other side, Iowa has had only unknown attackers in the past three years.

Zooming in on one specific city, St. Louis has been home to three attacks from the Black Hebrew Israelites in the span of three days in 2016. It's the greatest concentration of attacks in the three years.
